Destiny Visual DNA — Shader Pillars

Which of these are most important for Glasswake's feel? Select all that apply.

A

Dramatic Rim / Back Lighting

Destiny's signature look. Objects glow at their edges when backlit — especially characters against the sky. Creates a sense of epic scale and atmosphere. Your RimLight.ts already does a basic version of this, but Destiny's is stronger, warmer, and varies with time of day.

Current: Basic fresnel rim on MeshStandardMaterial, dusk boost
Destiny-level: Per-material rim intensity, colored rim from sky gradient, rim on terrain edges too
B

Atmospheric Perspective / Distance Haze

In Destiny, distant geometry doesn't just fade — it shifts color toward a soft atmospheric tint (cool blue in daylight, warm amber at dusk, deep violet at night). This replaces basic fog with depth-aware color grading that changes with lighting conditions.

Current: FogExp2 + screen-space FogVeil (no real depth)
Destiny-level: Depth-buffer-aware haze, sky-color-matched, biome-tinted
C

Selective Bloom + Emissive Accents

Destiny uses bloom sparingly on bright sources — sun, glowing mech eyes, energy weapons, loot drops. Not full-screen bloom haze, but targeted glow on emissive surfaces. Makes technology and magic feel alive.

Current: No bloom at all
Destiny-level: Threshold bloom pass, emissive channel on Walker mechs, campfire glow
D

Cinematic Color Grading

Destiny desaturates midtones while keeping highlights warm and shadows cool. Gives a painterly, filmic quality — not photorealistic, not cel-shaded. Your ToonRamp + BiomePalette already color-grade, but Destiny's approach is more about contrast curves and split-toning.

Current: Toon ramp (2-band) + biome palette tinting
Destiny-level: Lift/gamma/gain, split-toning (warm highlights / cool shadows), vignette, contrast S-curve
E

Contact Shadows / SSAO

Subtle darkening where objects meet surfaces — grass roots, mech feet on ground, rocks against terrain. Adds grounding and depth that makes the world feel solid instead of floaty.

Current: Basic vertex AO on terrain slopes, nothing on objects
Destiny-level: Screen-space AO (N8AO or custom), contact shadow for sun
F

Epic Skybox + Volumetric Clouds

Destiny's skies are dramatic — layered cloud decks, god rays punching through, nebulae and color gradients. Your CloudDome + GodRays are a start, but Destiny's clouds have real volume, self-shadowing, and silver lining effects.

Current: CloudDome (fbm on sphere) + screen-space god rays
Destiny-level: Multi-layer clouds, volumetric raymarching (perf-gated), dramatic gradients

Performance note: Each pillar has a performance cost. A, B, D are cheap (shader-only). C and F are moderate (extra render passes). E is the most expensive (screen-space sampling). We'll tier everything so low-end still runs smooth.
