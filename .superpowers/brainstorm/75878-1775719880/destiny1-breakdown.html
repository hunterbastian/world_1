Destiny 1 Visual Identity — Full Breakdown

Beyond rim lighting, what makes Destiny 1 look like Destiny 1? Select the pillars you want to bring into Mech World.

A

Atmospheric Perspective

Destiny 1's most recognizable trait. Distant geometry shifts toward a colored haze that matches the sky — not uniform fog, but a gradient that's warm near the horizon and cool overhead. The Cosmodrome's blue-grey distance, Venus's golden haze, the Moon's stark black falloff. Each destination had its own atmospheric palette.

Replaces your current FogVeil screen-space approach with depth-aware, sky-matched atmospheric scattering.
B

Filmic Color Grading

Destiny 1 had a specific color science: desaturated midtones, warm highlights, cool shadows. Not cel-shaded, not photorealistic — somewhere in between. A subtle S-curve on contrast, slight vignette, and split-toning that made everything feel cinematic. Your ToonRamp is more anime than Destiny — this would replace it with a filmic grade.

Replaces ToonRampEffect + BiomePaletteEffect with a unified CinematicGradeEffect (lift/gamma/gain, split-tone, vignette).
C

Stylized PBR Materials

Destiny 1 used physically-based rendering but pushed it toward painterly. Metals had exaggerated specular highlights, cloth was soft and diffuse, organic materials had subtle subsurface scatter. The key: materials told you what something was made of at a glance. Your terrain uses vertex colors + noise — this would add material identity.

Enhance terrain shader with material-aware zones (rock=rough specular, grass=soft diffuse, snow=high scatter). Mech materials get metallic + emissive channels.
D

God Rays + Selective Bloom

Your god rays already exist but Destiny 1's were more volumetric and directional — light shafts that occluded behind terrain. Combined with selective bloom on bright surfaces (sun, emissive mech joints, campfire glow), this creates the "epic vista" moment Destiny was famous for.

Upgrade GodRaysEffect with depth occlusion. Add threshold bloom pass for emissive surfaces. Moderate perf cost.
E

Dramatic Sky & Cloud Upgrade

Destiny 1's skyboxes were painted works of art — layered cloud decks with silver linings, dramatic gradients from warm horizon to deep blue zenith, and the Traveler looming overhead. Your CloudDome is procedural noise on a sphere — this would add layered cloud types, silver-lining effect, and richer sky gradients.

Multi-layer cloud system, sky gradient LUT, cloud silver lining from sun angle. Moderate perf cost.
F

SSAO + Contact Shadows

Destiny 1 had strong ambient occlusion — dark creases, grounded objects, depth in crevices. Trees meeting terrain, mech feet on ground, rock formations against hillsides. This is what makes a world feel solid instead of objects floating on a surface.

Add N8AO (performant SSAO for Three.js) or custom screen-space AO. Most expensive option — quality-tiered.
